In Leslie Marmom Silko's novel Ceremony, there is an extreme emphasis on the usage of storytelling as part of ritual and ceremony in order to purify the main character Tayo. Throughout the books, myths and the book itself are being told in parallel and work to tell a story to the reader and makes the reader participate in Tayo's purification. The work is directly based off of Laguna Pueblo traditions as both Silko and Tayo are Laguna Pueblo and mixed-race.
